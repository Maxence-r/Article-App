Fake News & Lateral Reading

It is likely that the video of the snowboarder being chased by a bear is a hoax. Brown bears do not live in Japan, and the audio of fake growls added to the video further suggests that the video is not genuine. Additionally, the fact that the bear appears to be split in half and part of the image is missing indicates that the video has been edited or manipulated in some way. It is always important to be skeptical of videos and other information that we encounter online, and to verify their authenticity before believing or sharing them.

Lateral reading is a term that refers to the process of gathering information about a particular topic by looking at a variety of sources, rather than relying on just one source. This approach allows you to get a more well-rounded view of the topic at hand, and can help you to better understand the issue from different perspectives. By looking at multiple sources, you can also compare and contrast different viewpoints, which can help you to develop your own informed opinion on the topic. Lateral reading is an important skill for anyone who wants to gain a deeper understanding of a particular subject.
